Why Medical School?

Why Medical School?!

My Dad wanted me to do a professional degree. I liked the idea of being a professional. I always wanted to get into medical school I’d say. I didn’t like law after having to do a complementary unit in undergrad. I definitely didn’t have the chops for engineering; my relatives kept stressing the notion of avoiding reading engineering in university. Alas, I couldn’t get into medical school as an undergraduate. I had mucked around too much for my own good.

Was it for the money?

Initially, not really. After doing an internship, considering remuneration, all the more it reinforced my belief that a physician’s salary (most of the time) is commensurate of their degree.

Was it for the prestige?

This one hit me more squarely. The respect and being the first ones in the family to be able to get it were of great importance in my book.

Why not my previous industry?

I’ll be honest. I did only an internship. Never held an FTE position. But I knew it wasn’t for me.

I’ll add some other push factors:

My friends who are medical students or physicians already. They were instrumental in inspiring me to give it a go.
Cliche: Being able to help people.
