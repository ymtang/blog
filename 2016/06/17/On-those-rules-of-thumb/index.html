On those rules of thumb

The 3 A’s

During the road trip in Melbourne, Victoria, 大姐 was regaling some words of wisdom she had learnt from one of the tutors she had at Westmead.
As per the heading – the three A’s are as follows:

  1. Availability - Being available to catch opportunities that might arise
  2. Affable - Being able to get along with people (it’s a team environment)
  3. Average - Being able to do anything without messing up
    (Actually, I had to do a quick Google because I forgot the first A. Apparently the search results pointed to a TED talk)

The first few things that came to mind were: maxim, dogma, axiom and platitude
Here’re their definitions:

maxim - a short, pithy statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct.

dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.

axiom - a statement or proposition which is regarded as being established, accepted, or self-evidently true.

platitude - a remark or statement, especially one with a moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful.

First three were variations on a theme, last was probably a value judgement on my part (must be the cynic in me).
BUT it was still the first time I’d heard of the concept and what resonated with me was that I feel that I can live with 2/3 of the stipulations: Availability and Average
